Rumi's Guide to Personal Growth

Ancient wisdom for modern becoming.

This digital humanities project translates Rumi's teachings on self-awareness, pain, gratitude, purpose, and love into a modern personal growth blog. Each entry connects a Rumi-inspired idea to a contemporary challenge, explains the historical context, and ends with a practical reflection.

Why Rumi Today?

A historical voice for modern questions

Jalal al-Din Rumi was a 13th-century Persian poet, Islamic scholar, theologian, jurist, and Sufi mystic. His work continues to speak to questions students still face: Who am I becoming? How do I respond to pain? What is worth loving, serving, and practicing?

Research: We frame Rumi within his Islamic, Persian, Sufi, and historical context, including his migration, teaching life, and transformation after meeting Shams-e Tabrizi.
Psychology: The project connects Rumi's symbols to modern ideas such as self-awareness, emotional resilience, mindfulness, purpose, and post-traumatic growth.
Creativity: The site uses a growth map, interactive reflections, modern scenarios, and translation notes instead of random quote collecting.
Presentation: The pages are structured to support a clear 15-minute class flow.
