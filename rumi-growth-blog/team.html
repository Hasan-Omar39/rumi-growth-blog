Collaboration

Team Process

This page makes the group effort visible. The project works like a small editorial board: one team checks Rumi's context, one translates ideas into modern psychology, one shapes the visual mood, and one keeps the website and presentation coherent.

Our Group Process

The project was created through an asynchronous workflow: research notes first, post outlines second, peer review third, then design, coding, editing, and presentation rehearsal. This keeps the tone consistent even though multiple students contribute.

How we kept one voice

The group used a simple editorial standard so the website would not feel like disconnected pieces.

Source Check

Every Rumi idea is labeled as context, paraphrase, inspired teaching, or source-dependent quotation.

Peer Review

Each post is reviewed for clarity, tone, historical respect, and whether the reflection activity is useful.

Presentation Fit

Sections are written so speakers can explain the project in a clear 15-minute order.
