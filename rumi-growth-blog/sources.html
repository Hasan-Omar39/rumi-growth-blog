Translation Note

Because Rumi's poetry is often translated, paraphrased, and misattributed, this project treats quotations carefully. When a translator or source is known, it is credited. When a phrase is uncertain, it is identified as commonly attributed rather than presented as a verified direct quotation.

Blog quote cards use Rumi-inspired teachings or clearly marked paraphrases.
Direct quotations should include translator, source work, and, when possible, original Persian or a transliteration.
The group avoids stripping Rumi's Islamic and Persian context away for generic self-help language.
